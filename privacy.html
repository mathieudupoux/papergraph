Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 3, 2025

TL;DR: Papergraph is privacy-first. Your research data is stored in your personal Supabase account. You maintain full control and ownership. We don't collect, sell, or share your personal information.

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy governs the manner in which Papergraph ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, maintains, and discloses information collected from users ("you" or "your") of the Papergraph application. This policy applies to all services offered by Papergraph.

2. Information Collection and Use

Papergraph requires user authentication to provide secure access to the application. The following information is collected and processed:

2.1 Account Information

To use Papergraph, you must create an account through one of our supported authentication providers:

  • GitHub OAuth
  • Google OAuth
  • Email/Password authentication via Supabase Auth

During authentication, we collect your email address and a unique user identifier. This information is used solely for account management and access control purposes.

2.2 Research Data

All user-generated content, including but not limited to research articles, connections, tags, project metadata, and related materials, is stored in your personal Supabase database instance. This data is:

  • Stored exclusively in your personal cloud database
  • Protected by Supabase Row Level Security (RLS) policies
  • Accessible only to you through your authenticated account
  • Not accessed, processed, or stored by Papergraph on any central servers

3. Data Storage and Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: All data transmission between your browser and our services uses HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • Access Control: Database-level Row Level Security ensures data isolation between users
  • Authentication: Secure session management through Supabase Auth with token-based authentication
  • Infrastructure: Leveraging Supabase's enterprise-grade infrastructure and security practices

4. Cookies and Tracking

Papergraph uses essential cookies strictly necessary for authentication and session management. We do not use cookies for advertising, tracking, or analytics purposes.

  • Session Cookies: Required for maintaining authenticated sessions
  • Authentication Tokens: Used to verify user identity and secure API requests

We do not use third-party analytics tools or tracking scripts. No behavioral data is collected or transmitted to external services.

5. Third-Party Service Providers

Papergraph relies on the following third-party service providers to deliver our services:

5.1 Supabase

We use Supabase as our backend infrastructure provider for authentication and database services. Supabase processes user authentication data and stores project data in accordance with their privacy policy. For more information, please review the Supabase Privacy Policy.

5.2 OAuth Providers

When you choose to authenticate using GitHub or Google OAuth, you are subject to the respective privacy policies of these providers:

  • GitHub Privacy Statement
  • Google Privacy Policy

We only receive the minimum information necessary for authentication (email and user identifier) from these providers.

6. Your Rights and Data Control

As a user of Papergraph, you retain full ownership and control over your data. You have the following rights:

6.1 Right to Access

You may access all of your stored data at any time through the application interface. Additionally, you can export your complete research dataset in multiple formats including JSON, BibTeX, and PDF.

6.2 Right to Deletion

You have the right to delete your account and all associated data. This can be accomplished through your Supabase dashboard. Upon deletion, all your project data, including articles, connections, and metadata, will be permanently removed from the database.

6.3 Right to Data Portability

All data stored in Papergraph is exportable in industry-standard formats, ensuring you can migrate your research data to other platforms if desired.

7. Data Retention

Your data is retained for as long as your account remains active. If you delete your account, all associated data will be permanently deleted from our systems. We do not retain user data after account deletion.

8. Children's Privacy

Papergraph is not directed to individuals under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete such information promptly.

9. Open Source Transparency

Papergraph is open source software licensed under the MIT License. The complete source code is publicly available on GitHub, allowing independent verification of our privacy practices and data handling procedures.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy at any time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage users to frequently check this page for any changes to stay informed about how we are protecting the personal information we collect. Your continued use of the service after any modifications to this Privacy Policy will constitute your acknowledgment of the modifications and your consent to abide by the modified Privacy Policy.

11. Contact Information

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data handling practices, you may contact us through the following channels:

  • Submit an issue on our GitHub repository
  • Contact the maintainer directly through GitHub: @remyvallot

We will make every effort to respond to your inquiry in a timely manner.

Remember: Papergraph requires an account to ensure secure access to your personal cloud database. Your data is yours, and we're committed to keeping it that way.
