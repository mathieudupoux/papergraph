Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 14, 2026

In Short: Papergraph is an open-source browser tool. When you sign in with Google, we access your email, username, and profile picture to create your account. All your data is securely stored in Supabase. We don't track you, sell your data, or use it for advertising.

What is Papergraph

Papergraph is an open-source tool that runs in your browser. We provide the software that helps you organize and visualize research articles, creating a knowledge graph of your academic work.

Data we collect

Papergraph collects and stores the following information:

When you sign in with Google OAuth:

  • Email address: Used to create and identify your account
  • Username: Displayed in your profile
  • Profile picture: Displayed in your profile

When you use Papergraph:

  • Project Data: Your research projects, including article metadata, connections, and visual organization
  • User Preferences: Application settings and customizations
  • Account Information: Authentication tokens and session data

If you sign in with GitHub or email, we collect similar account information (email, username, profile picture) through those authentication providers.

How we use your data

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: To create and manage your Papergraph account
  • User Identification: To associate your projects and data with your account
  • Profile Display: To show your username and profile picture in the application interface
  • Core Functionality: To save, sync, and display your research projects and articles

We do not:

  • Use your data for advertising or marketing
  • Share your information with third parties
  • Track your activity outside of core application functionality
  • Sell or monetize your personal information

Papergraph's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

How we store and protect your data

All your data is stored securely in Supabase databases with the following protections:

  • Encryption: Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest
  • Row Level Security: Database policies ensure only you can access your data
  • Isolated Storage: Your projects and information are isolated from other users
  • No Developer Access: Papergraph developers cannot access your personal information or projects

Third-party services

Supabase provides our authentication and database infrastructure. When you use Papergraph, Supabase:

  • Handles account creation and login (GitHub, Google, or email authentication)
  • Stores your projects, articles, and connections in encrypted databases
  • Manages security through Row Level Security policies

Supabase processes your data according to their Privacy Policy. Your data remains yours and is protected by strict access controls.

What we don't do

  • We don't use analytics or advertising cookies
  • We don't track your browsing activity
  • We don't sell or monetize your personal information
  • We don't share your data with third parties (except Supabase for infrastructure)
  • We don't access your data for any purpose other than providing the service

Your rights and control

Export Your Data: You can export all your projects anytime in JSON, BibTeX, or PDF format using the export functionality in the application.

Delete Your Account: You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time through your profile settings. This action is irreversible.

Access and Correction: You can view and edit all your data directly in the application. You have full control over your projects and information.

If you need assistance with your data or account, please contact us through the channels listed below.

Open source transparency

Papergraph is open source under the MIT License. You can review the complete source code on GitHub to verify exactly how your data is handled and what the application does.
